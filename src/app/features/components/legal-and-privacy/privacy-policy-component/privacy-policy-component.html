2. Overview of Processing

The following summary outlines the types of data we process and the purposes for doing so.

Types of data processed

  • Usage data — IP address, request timestamps, daily request count
  • Log data — Server logs including IP address, browser type, referring URL
  • Content data — Recipe preferences and inputs you provide to generate recipes
  • Cookbook data — Generated recipes you save to your personal cookbook

Purposes of processing

  • Providing and operating the online service
  • Enforcing a daily request limit (3 requests per user per day) to prevent abuse
  • Generating AI-powered recipe suggestions based on your inputs
  • Storing recipes in your personal cookbook for later access
  • Tracking likes via an anonymous UUID to enable favorites without a login
  • Ensuring the technical security and availability of the platform

Categories of data subjects

  • Website visitors and users of the recipe generation service

3. Legal Bases (GDPR)

We process personal data on the following legal bases under the GDPR:

  • Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — Legitimate Interests: We process your IP address and request metadata to enforce a fair-use limit of 3 requests per day. This is necessary to protect the service from abuse and ensure availability for all users. Our legitimate interest outweighs the minimal privacy impact of storing an IP address for a short period.
  • Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — Performance of a Contract: When you use the recipe generation feature, we process your inputs (ingredients, preferences) to deliver the requested service.

In addition to GDPR, the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG) applies where relevant, in particular regarding the right to information, deletion, and objection.

4. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, loss, or misuse, in accordance with Art. 32 GDPR.

All data transmitted between your browser and our services is encrypted using TLS/SSL (HTTPS). Access to databases and processing systems is restricted and protected by authentication controls.

5. International Data Transfers

Some service providers are based outside the European Economic Area (EEA), in particular in the United States. When personal data is transferred to these countries, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place as required by Chapter V GDPR.

Transfers to the US are based on the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) where the provider is certified, or on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) pursuant to Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR.

Affected providers: Vercel Inc. (hosting/CDN) and Groq Inc. (AI inference). Details are listed in the sections below.

6. Storage & Deletion

Personal data is deleted or anonymized as soon as the purpose for which it was collected no longer applies, unless statutory retention obligations require longer storage.

  • Rate-limiting logs (IP address + request count): Retained on a rolling 24-hour basis. Records older than 24 hours are deleted automatically.
  • Server access logs (IONOS / Vercel): Retained for up to 7 days for security and diagnostic purposes, then deleted automatically.
  • Recipe inputs processed by Groq: Sent to the Groq API for inference only. We do not store recipe request content beyond the duration of the request. Groq's own retention practices apply (see their Privacy Policy linked below).

9. Rate Limiting & Database

To ensure fair use and protect the service from abuse, we enforce a limit of 3 AI recipe requests per user per day. To implement this, we log your IP address, the number of requests made, and the timestamp of each request in a managed database.

No other personal data (such as names or account information) is collected for this purpose. Log entries are automatically deleted on a rolling 24-hour basis.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — Legitimate interests. Storing an IP address for rate-limiting is necessary to prevent abuse and ensure equal access for all users.

11. Cookbook & Recipe Storage

When you save a recipe to your cookbook, the generated recipe data (title, ingredients, instructions, and your assigned preferences) is stored persistently in our database so you can access it on future visits.

Recipes are linked to an anonymous identifier stored in your browser's LocalStorage (see section 12). No name, email address, or other personally identifiable information is required or collected for this purpose.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — Processing necessary to provide the explicitly requested feature (saving and retrieving your cookbook).

Stored recipes are associated with your anonymous UUID and remain in the database until you delete them or request their removal. To request deletion, contact: contact@maik-groth.com

12. Likes & Local Storage

When you like a recipe, we generate a random anonymous identifier (UUID) and store it in your browser's LocalStorage. This UUID is then stored in our database together with the liked recipe.

The UUID has no connection to your name, email address, or any other personal data. Its sole purpose is to prevent duplicate likes and to restore your favorites when you return to the site — without requiring a login or account.

Legal basis: § 25(2)(2) TTDSG — Storage is strictly necessary to provide the feature explicitly requested by the user. No consent banner is required.

You can remove the UUID at any time by clearing your browser's LocalStorage for this site. The associated like entries in our database can be deleted on request: contact@maik-groth.com
